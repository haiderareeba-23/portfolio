Problem
  • Fenzo's homepage structure did not reflect the product's positioning as an AI course generator. There was no dedicated section explaining why Fenzo differs from catalog based platforms.
  • Competitive positioning was limited to a single comparison with ChatGPT, ignoring the broader competitive landscape of Coursera, Duolingo, Khan Academy, and Udemy that prospects actually evaluate against.
  • The page had no audience segmentation. Professionals such as researchers, nurses, and lawyers could not see themselves in the product, and there was no business positioning to capture the teams and enterprise segment.
  • Navigation was flat, with no way to surface top courses, product tiers, or resources. The standalone Community section added page length without conversion value, and there was no FAQ to handle common objections.
Gathered Insights
Competitive Analysis

Six criteria that matter to buyers

Analyzed competitor positioning across Coursera, Duolingo, Khan Academy, Udemy, and ChatGPT to identify six differentiating criteria: generation from the user's exact question, interactivity, personalization, topic breadth, time to first lesson, and structured progress tracking.

Pattern Research

Proven section structures

Reviewed reference patterns from best in class SaaS sites, such as Docebo's FAQ layout, to inform section structures that are proven to convert.

Page Audit

Preserve what already works

Audited the existing page to identify what was working: the query first hero, live interactive demos, the science backed credibility section, and the campus program. All of these assets were preserved intact.

Solution

A deliberate eleven section narrative, anchored by the line: every other product serves pre made content; Fenzo generates.

Built for Everyone audience grid

Six extensible tiles for Researchers, Nurses, Lawyers, Medical Students, Engineers, and Educators, plus a banner tile for learners of all kinds. Each tile carries role specific feature bullets and its own call to action.

Five competitor comparison board

Replaced the ChatGPT only comparison with a six criteria board across five competitors, rated honestly with yes, partial, and no states and per cell context notes.

Business section for L&D teams

Positioned Fenzo as the AI tool that enhances an existing learning program, with feature cards for document to course conversion, role paths, analytics, and single sign on with LMS integration, plus demo and sales buttons.

Navigation dropdowns

Three click to open menus: Explore for top courses, Products for individual and organization tiers, and Resources for Guides, Paths, Industries, and Community, folding the removed Community section into Resources.

Fen, the interactive fox mascot

Fixed to the page edge, delivering contextual tips as visitors scroll into each section, with mute and dismiss controls.

Objection handling FAQ

A six item accordion addressing the top objections: chatbot differentiation, account requirements, own document uploads, topic coverage, build time, and company or college use.

Implementation Process
  • Single file delivery. Everything ships as one self contained HTML file with inline CSS and JavaScript, so the page can be previewed directly in a browser with no build step.
  • Extended the existing design system. The coral, navy, and cream palette, Fraunces and Inter typography, the reusable fox mark, and the card and wave divider patterns were carried into all seven new components so they look native to the brand.
  • Lightweight vanilla JavaScript interactions. Click toggled dropdowns with outside click and Escape handling, an animated FAQ accordion, and a visibility observer that drives the mascot's contextual tips per section.
  • Verified the final build. Balanced markup across all eleven sections, no broken anchors or duplicate identifiers, and JavaScript validated with a syntax check.
Handling Challenges

Preserving design language while doubling the page

Reused existing tokens, card patterns, and section rhythms so the new sections read as original parts of the site rather than additions.

Making the audience grid easy to extend

Built tiles as a repeatable card pattern with an icon, a blurb, three feature bullets, and a call to action, so new roles can be added by copying one block while the grid reflows automatically.

Honest competitive comparison

Used a three state rating system with per cell context notes, conceding competitor strengths such as Duolingo's drills and Khan Academy's mastery map, keeping the board credible while the criteria still favor generation.

Contextual mascot without intrusiveness

Scoped tips to section visibility thresholds, hid bubbles automatically after nine seconds, and added persistent mute and dismiss controls so the mascot guides rather than nags.

Section Requirements

Every section of the redesign was specified with an action item, a support argument, functional requirements, a process, and an intended output. Expand each one below.

Action item

Rebuild the top navigation with three dropdown menus named Explore, Products, and Resources, and keep the primary Start learning free button visible at every scroll position.

Support argument

The old flat navigation hid the depth of the product, so visitors had no fast route to top courses, product tiers, or supporting resources, which lengthened the path to conversion and buried the Community content.

Functional requirements

Each dropdown must open on click, close when the visitor clicks anywhere outside it or presses the Escape key, and allow only one menu to stay open at a time. The Explore menu lists the top courses, the Products menu lists the individual and organization tiers, and the Resources menu holds Guides, Paths, Industries, and Community. Every menu item must link to a valid anchor or page.

Process and steps

First map every destination the page needs to expose, then group those destinations into the three menus, then build one reusable dropdown component in vanilla JavaScript, then wire the open, close, and keyboard behaviors, and finally click through every link to confirm each target resolves.

Output aimed to achieve

Visitors can reach any part of the product in two clicks, the signup button is always available, and the Community content survives inside Resources even though its standalone page section was removed.

Action item

Preserve the query first hero with the headline The perfect course, every time, together with its question input, its Create button, and its document upload control.

Support argument

The hero already proved the core promise by letting visitors type a question and receive a course, so it was the strongest converting element on the page and replacing it would have destroyed existing value.

Functional requirements

The section needs the headline, a supporting line, a free text field that accepts any question, a Create button that submits the question, and an upload control that accepts the visitor's own documents. The section must remain the first thing on the page with nothing loading above it.

Process and steps

Audit the existing hero, confirm the input, the Create button, and the upload control still behave correctly after the surrounding page was restructured, and lock the section in as position one of the eleven part narrative.

Output aimed to achieve

A first time visitor understands within seconds that Fenzo generates a course from their exact question and can act on that understanding immediately.

Action item

Keep the interactive demo section titled One question in directly beneath the hero, with its working lesson previews.

Support argument

Proof should follow promise, and an interactive sample lesson is more persuasive than any amount of descriptive copy about interactivity.

Functional requirements

The section must present live canvas demonstrations, including the graph, the Riemann sum, and the atom visualizations, each of which responds to visitor input inside the page without loading external resources.

Process and steps

Verify each canvas demo still initializes after the restructure, confirm the demos respond to interaction, and keep the section in position two so momentum from the hero carries straight into proof.

Output aimed to achieve

Visitors experience the product's interactivity firsthand before reading a single claim about it.

Action item

Add a new section titled Not another course catalog that explains why a course generator differs from every catalog based platform.

Support argument

The old page never articulated the category difference, so visitors mentally filed Fenzo alongside course libraries and judged it by catalog size, a comparison it cannot win and should not enter.

Functional requirements

The section needs a clear headline, supporting copy that contrasts generation with pre made catalogs, and visual cards that each carry one differentiator so the argument can be scanned in seconds.

Process and steps

Distill the positioning into a small set of differentiators, write one card per differentiator using the existing card pattern, and place the section immediately after the demo so the visitor reads the explanation right after experiencing the product.

Output aimed to achieve

Visitors leave the section understanding that Fenzo builds a course from their question rather than serving them someone else's syllabus.

Action item

Build an audience grid titled Whatever you do, there's a course for you, with six role tiles for Researchers, Nurses, Lawyers, Medical Students, Engineers, and Educators, plus a banner tile for learners of all kinds. Give every tile its own explore button, such as Explore nursing courses, along with a Browse all courses button for the full catalog.

Support argument

Professionals could not see themselves in the old page, and a visitor who recognizes their own role in a product converts at a far higher rate than one reading generic copy.

Functional requirements

Each tile needs an icon, a short blurb, three role specific feature bullets, and a button that links to that role's courses. The grid must reflow automatically at every screen width, and adding a new role must require nothing more than copying one tile block.

Process and steps

Define the six launch roles, design one repeatable tile pattern, populate each tile with role specific copy, add the banner tile so no visitor feels excluded, and test the grid at mobile, tablet, and desktop widths.

Output aimed to achieve

Every visitor finds a tile that describes them within one scroll, and the team can add new audiences later without touching layout code.

Action item

Replace the ChatGPT only comparison with a board titled See how Fenzo stacks up that rates Fenzo against Coursera, Duolingo, Khan Academy, Udemy, and ChatGPT across six criteria, and close the section with a Start learning free button.

Support argument

Prospects evaluate Fenzo against the whole learning market rather than against one chatbot, so a single competitor comparison answered a question nobody was asking while ignoring the ones they were.

Functional requirements

The board needs six rows for the criteria: generation from the exact question, interactivity, personalization, topic breadth, time to first lesson, and structured progress tracking. Every cell must show one of three honest states, meaning yes, partial, or no, with a short context note. Competitor strengths such as Duolingo's drills and Khan Academy's mastery map must be conceded openly.

Process and steps

Research each competitor's actual capabilities, choose criteria that matter to buyers rather than criteria that merely flatter Fenzo, fill every cell with a rating and a note, anchor the section with the line that every other product serves pre made content while Fenzo generates, and end with the signup button.

Output aimed to achieve

A skeptical prospect trusts the board because it concedes real competitor strengths, yet still walks away convinced that generation is the capability only Fenzo offers.

Action item

Preserve the section titled Backed by the science of learning exactly as it was, in its new position after the comparison board.

Support argument

The credibility content was already working, and evidence based claims land best right after a competitive argument because they ground the marketing in research.

Functional requirements

The section must keep its research backed claims, its existing visual treatment, and its original copy without modification.

Process and steps

Move the section into position six of the narrative, confirm its styling survived the move, and verify its internal anchors still resolve.

Output aimed to achieve

Visitors who need rational justification for an emotional decision find peer reviewed grounding at exactly the moment doubt would otherwise creep in.

Action item

Add a business section titled Upgrade your L&D training program with Fenzo, with a Book a demo button and a Talk to sales button.

Support argument

The old page spoke only to individuals, leaving the teams and enterprise segment, which carries the largest contract values, with no entry point at all.

Functional requirements

The section must position Fenzo as the AI tool that enhances an existing learning program, and it needs four feature cards covering conversion of company documents into courses, role based learning paths, team analytics, and single sign on with LMS integration. Both buttons must route to their respective contact flows.

Process and steps

Write positioning that complements rather than threatens an L&D manager's existing program, build the four feature cards from the established card pattern, place the two buttons beneath them, and test both button destinations.

Output aimed to achieve

An L&D decision maker sees a credible enterprise offering and has two clear ways to start a conversation, one self serve and one through sales.

Action item

Preserve the college program section titled Bring Fenzo to your college, with its Bring Fenzo to your campus button and its Get in touch contact link.

Support argument

The campus program was an existing asset with its own audience, and removing it would have severed a working acquisition channel for student ambassadors and institutions.

Functional requirements

The section must retain its program description, its application button, and a visible contact email for institutional inquiries.

Process and steps

Carry the section over intact, slot it after the business section so the institutional audiences sit together, and confirm the application button and the email link both work.

Output aimed to achieve

Students and college administrators retain a direct route into the campus program without any loss of existing functionality.

Action item

Preserve the social proof section titled Loved by curious minds everywhere in its new position near the end of the page.

Support argument

Social proof is most effective after the product case has been fully made, because at that point testimonials confirm a decision the visitor is already leaning toward.

Functional requirements

The section must display the existing testimonial cards with names and roles, styled consistently with the rest of the page.

Process and steps

Move the section to position nine, confirm the cards render correctly, and check that the section flows naturally into the FAQ that follows it.

Output aimed to achieve

Visitors on the verge of converting receive final reassurance from voices like their own.

Action item

Add a six item accordion titled Frequently asked questions in which every question row is a button that expands its answer.

Support argument

Common objections were going unanswered on the page, forcing interested visitors to leave and search elsewhere. A Docebo style accordion is a proven pattern for resolving objections without adding visual length.

Functional requirements

The six questions must cover how Fenzo differs from a chatbot, whether an account is required, whether visitors can upload their own documents, what topics are covered, how long a course takes to build, and whether companies and colleges can use it. Each row must animate open and closed, and only the clicked row should toggle.

Process and steps

Collect the objections that appear most often, write one direct answer per question, build the accordion with an animated height transition, and test that every row opens and closes independently.

Output aimed to achieve

The most common reasons to hesitate are resolved on the page itself, moments before the final call to action.

Action item

Close the page with a short final section whose single button reads Ask your first question and returns the visitor to the hero input.

Support argument

A visitor who has read the entire narrative should never have to scroll back up unaided to act, so the page must end where the product begins.

Functional requirements

The section needs one line of closing copy and one prominent button that scrolls the visitor to the hero question field.

Process and steps

Write the closing line, style the button as the page's most prominent element, and confirm the scroll target lands the visitor with the input in view.

Output aimed to achieve

The narrative ends with a single unmistakable action, converting accumulated interest into a first generated course.

Action item

Add an interactive fox mascot named Fen, fixed to the page edge, that offers a contextual tip as the visitor scrolls into each section, with a mute button and a dismiss button.

Support argument

A long page benefits from a guide, and the brand's existing fox mark gives the mascot instant recognition while contextual tips keep visitors oriented through eleven sections.

Functional requirements

The mascot must detect which section is in view using a visibility observer, show the matching tip once the section is roughly a third visible, hide each tip automatically after nine seconds, and honor the mute and dismiss controls for the rest of the visit.

Process and steps

Write one short tip per section, attach a visibility observer to every section, tune the visibility threshold so tips fire neither early nor late, add the automatic hide timer, and add the persistent mute and dismiss controls.

Output aimed to achieve

The mascot guides visitors through the page like a friendly docent while never trapping anyone who prefers to browse in silence.
